Maintenance Procedure

Phase 1 — Workspace Preparation and Power Isolation
Prepared controlled maintenance environment to prevent contamination and hardware damage.
Actions performed:
• Powered down system completely
• Disconnected AC power cable
• Disconnected all external peripherals:
Keyboard
Mouse
Display cables
Ethernet cable
USB devices
Audio devices
Prepared workspace:
• Clean, dry, stable surface
• Dust-free maintenance area
• Adequate lighting for precision work
Tools and materials used:
• ESD-safe cleaning brushes
• Cotton swabs
• Cotton balls
• High-concentration medicinal alcohol (~90%)
• Lint-free cleaning materials

Note:

Isopropyl alcohol (IPA 99%) is the preferred industry standard; medicinal alcohol (~90%) was used safely and effectively due to availability constraints.

Phase 2 — Internal Access and Inspection
Ensured safe internal access.
Steps performed:
	1. Verified complete system shutdown
	2. Disconnected PSU from AC source
	3. Pressed power button briefly to discharge residual capacitors
	4. Removed side panel
	5. Conducted visual inspection of internal components
Verified:
• No liquid contamination
• No loose components
• No cable damage

Phase 3 — Internal Cleaning Procedure
Performed controlled internal dust removal.
Dry cleaning phase:
Used ESD-safe brushes to remove dust from:
• CPU cooler
• Heatsink fins
• Case interior surfaces
• Fan blades
• Motherboard surfaces
• Power supply intake areas
Precision cleaning phase:
Used cotton swabs lightly moistened with alcohol to clean:
• Fan blade edges
• Contact surfaces
• Accessible metal surfaces
• Non-sensitive structural areas
Precautions observed:
• Avoided oversaturation
• Prevented liquid exposure to sensitive circuitry
• Allowed full evaporation prior to reassembly

Phase 4 — CPU Thermal Compound Removal
Removed existing thermal compound.
Steps performed:
	1. Removed CPU cooler assembly
	2. Exposed CPU heat spreader surface
	3. Removed old thermal compound using lint-free materials and alcohol-moistened swabs
Ensured complete removal from:
• CPU heat spreader
• Cooler contact surface
Verified:
• Clean, residue-free surfaces
• No particulate contamination

Phase 5 — Thermal Compound Application
Applied new thermal compound to CPU.
CPU Model: Intel Core i9-10900K
Procedure:
	1. Applied appropriate quantity of thermal compound to CPU center
	2. Ensured correct volume (no over-application)
	3. Reinstalled CPU cooler assembly
Ensured:
• Even pressure distribution
• Secure mounting
• Proper cooler alignment
Thermal interface integrity restored.

Phase 6 — System Reassembly and Validation
Reassembled system components.
Steps performed:
	1. Reinstalled case side panel
	2. Reconnected power cable
	3. Reconnected peripherals
	4. Powered system on
Verified successful boot.
